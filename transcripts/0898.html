Lecture Transcription

We are reading today from Srimad-Bhagavatam Canto-six, Chapter-seven, entitled- 'Indra offends Bṛhaspati', Sloka number,Twenty-four [coughs and clears throat]

Crucial principles of bhakti

Many crucial lessons are to be learnt in this particular narration of Sri Sukhadev Goswami, one of which is that Vaishnava etiquette, it is not just a detail, in Krishna consciousness culture, it is crucial, essential, and our spiritual lives depend on it. Oftentimes, devotees have the tendency to make wrong priorities. What is essential, appears to be details, that could either be accepted or rejected according to our convenience, and sometimes what is details, is imagined to be something essential. We know that our sadhana, our bhajan, is crucial to our progress. Srila Rupa Goswami, he explains five principles, of the sixty-four principles, that are most essential for spiritual progress. To associate with Vaishnavas, to chant the holy names of the Lord, to study Srimad-Bhagavatam, to worship the deity, the archa murti and to live in a holy place like Vrindavan, Navadvip, Jagannath Puri, and also worshipping Tulasi, is sometimes within that category. So, to chant the holy names is essential.

harer nāma harer nāma
harer nāmaiva kevalam
kalau nāsty eva nāsty eva
nāsty eva gatir anyathā

[Cc Madhya 6.242]

[In this age of quarrel and hypocrisy, the only means of deliverance is the chanting of the holy names of the Lord. There is no other way. There is no other way. There is no other way.]

'In this age of Kali, it is the most powerful means of deliverance, but if you do not follow proper Vaishnava etiquette, our chanting will have minimal effect.' Lord Chaitanya said, "Simply chant the holy names, and do not offend others, and Krishna will be there to save you." In fact, Chaitanya Mahaprabhu said, "Krishna is non different than his name, namnam akari bahudha nija-sarva-shaktis [Sri Śikṣāṣṭaka 02] all of his powers are within his name." But then the Lord said, if a devotee is chanting the holy names, but offends Vaishnavas, Krishna is so displeased, that Krishna through his name may destroy you, out of mercy. Krishna's name is Krishna. He is a person. It is not something mechanical, we chant, but we must chant in a way that pleases the holy name. The seventh offense to the holy name is to commit sinful activities on the strength of chanting. If we think let me chant, and then I can do anything, sinful and then I will rectify by chanting again, if that is our mentality, you are offending God, in his name, and the first, of all offenses is to blaspheme the devotees of the Lord. Why are these offenses? It is not just something mechanical. It must be understand [understood] in a very personalized way. These are offenses because they displease Krishna, substantially. Thus, Lord Chaitanya taught how we should chant the holy name

tṛṇād api su-nīcena
taror iva sahiṣṇunā
amāninā māna-dena
kīrtanīyaḥ sadā hariḥ

[Cc Adi 17.31, Śikṣāṣṭaka 3]

[One who thinks himself lower than the grass, who is more tolerant than a tree, and who does not expect personal honor yet is always prepared to give all respect to others can very easily always chant the holy name of the Lord]

'To be humble like a blade of grass, tolerant like a tree, ready to offer all respect to others, and expect none in return. In this way we can chant the holy names of the Lord constantly'. Srimad-Bhagavatam,

How to Worship the Deities?

Just by studying Srimad-Bhagavatam, we may not develop a taste within the heart. There are many scholars of the Bhagavata Purana, who intellectually saturate themselves, but the message does not transform their heart. Why? Because Srimad-Bhagavatam explains, to get a taste for the Bhagavatam, you must observe the proper etiquette, you must serve great souls. That service must come from the heart, with humility, with the desire to please, with care, not to offend. Then Krishna is pleased with our disposition. And as we hear Srimad-Bhagavatam or speak Srimad- Bhagavatam, Krishna manifests himself within our hearts. And the worship of the deity, without proper etiquette, we will not get the proper result. We may even get an opposite result. If we offend the deity, we will not make progress. The deities, Radharani and Krishna, they are there, standing before us, in order to do proper puja-seva we must understand, what they want and what they don't want, personally. This is a universal principle. In the Christian religion Jesus Christ said, "If you are coming to bring in all, bring in an offering to the altar, but you have an offensive dispute with someone else, leave your offering, go back, resolve that dispute and then and come and offer to the deity." A kanishtha adhikari, according to Srimad Bhagavatam is the lowest platform one who worships the deity, but does not offer honor, respect, and service to the devotees, that is devotional service, but it is on a lower platform, because Krishna says, "mad-bhakta-pūjābhyadhikā [SB. 19.11.20-24] worship of my devotee is what pleases me the most." If we offend the devotees, the Vaishnavas, then do you think Krishna would like to see us, when we come to worship him on the altar? Not at all. So, worship of the deities is one of the five most important principles of bhakti, according to our Gaudiya Vaishnava system. But it is crucial, it is essential to have proper etiquette, in dealings with others, so the deity is pleased.

Dhama Apradhas

And living in a holy dhama, of Brajabhumi there are naam-aparadhas. There are dhama-aparadhas. It is said that it is the holy land that reveals the holy dhama, because you cannot see Vrindavan with material eyes. premāñjana-cchurita-bhakti-vilocanena [Brahma-saṁhitā 5.38] Krishna is divine, he is transcendental and similarly his abode. The holy dhama is transcendental; it cannot be approached, through our mundane senses. The holy dhama is non- different than Radha Gopinath. The holy Dhama reveals herself, according to how we please her. Vrindavaneshwari, Radharani is the queen of Vrindavan, if she is pleased, she reveals Vrindavan to us. It is not a matter of buying a ticket and going there, and going to shop in Loi bazaar, and we're only seeing the superficial external covering of the holy dhama, which is also holy, and will sanctify us but if we actually want to see as it is, to the extent, and to the degree that Srimati Radharani is pleased, she will reveal the holy dhama to us. And how do we please Sri Radha? It is not according to our imagination. We must understand, from the spiritual masters, from the sadhus, from the shastras, and if we follow those principles carefully, then only will we understand what is Vrindavan.

I remember in 1971, some devotees had an argument with… actually it happened a little later, some of the devotees had an argument with some Brajawasi merchants. So, in this way, Vaishnava etiquette is essential, and here in the Srimad-Bhagavatam, which is the topmost scripture, the Amalam Puranam, teaching the highest understanding of the Absolute Truth, culminates in Krishna's Braja Lila is explaining so emphatically, through this example today.

To know that everything is the property of Krishna, I am nothing, my wealth, my knowledge, my abilities, my very life, everything is the property of Krishna. If we understand this principle, there is no possibility of becoming proud. janasya moho 'yam ahaṁ mameti [SB.5.5.8] the very root of material bondage are these two misconceptions, I and mine. So, Indra on this particular day is sitting in this assembly, on his throne, with his beautiful wife Saci beside him, and all the variety of devatas, and even the great sages and rishis, are worshipping him. Krishna Following Vaishnava Etiquette

Vaishnava etiquette is something very serious, and therefore, the Supreme Lord as well as his devotees, teach us this principle, amāninā māna-dena [Cc Ādi 17.31, Śikṣāṣṭaka 3] to offer respect to others. Srila Prabhupada herein quotes that Krishna, he is full of all six opulence's. Whatever Indra or anyone else has is only by the grace of Krishna. He is the Supreme Lord, he is jagat-pate - the Lord of the universe, and when he was in his palace in Dwaraka, when Narada Muni came in to the assembly, Krishna came down, offered his throne, to offer all respect, honor and worship Narada Muni. Narada Muni knows that he is the Supreme Lord, I am only his devotee, but still, Prabhupada says, because he was a brahmachari, and a Brahman, and a realized sage, Krishna, wanted to show the world by his example. In Bhagavad-gita Krishna tells, "If I do not set the proper example by my standards, then the whole world will be ruined because they will not know." Similarly, Sudama Vipra, he wasn't an inter… inter universal celebrated sages, like Narada Rishi. Sudama Vipra was just an unknown Brahman. Just living in Porbander, outside of Dwaraka.
